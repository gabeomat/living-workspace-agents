# Sales Call Copilot

**The sales team you never had, working every call you take. It briefs you before, debriefs you after, drafts the follow-up within the hour, coaches you honestly against a fixed rubric — and never forgets who's waiting on what.**

Solo sellers rarely lose deals on the call. They lose them in the gaps: the follow-up that went out three days late, the objection nobody wrote down, the promised proposal that slipped, the same price-delivery stumble repeated for months because nobody was watching. Sales Call Copilot closes the gaps — and it's the only sales coach that has actually heard all your calls.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it learns how you sell.** A quick interview captures your offers and real prices (proposals will only ever quote these), how a deal usually goes, your known objections, and how your calls get recorded. Deals already in flight get captured too, so your pipeline is honest on day one.
2. **After every call: drop the transcript in, say "debrief my call."** You get a structured debrief (their goals in their words, objections raised, commitments both ways), an honest scorecard against seven fixed dimensions — talk ratio, discovery depth, objection handling, price delivery, confidence leaks, the ask, next-step clarity — each score citing the transcript, ending with **one thing** to do differently next call. Plus the follow-up email, drafted in *your* voice, referencing what they actually said — built to be approved and sent within the hour. (Transcript reading runs on a lightweight model; the heavy thinking is saved for where it matters.)
3. **Before your next call: "brief me for my call with X."** One page from the deal's own history: what they said last time, open loops, likely objections, which offer this routes to, and the single goal of the call. Nobody walks in cold.
4. **When a call earns it: "draft the proposal."** Built from the prospect's own recorded words — their goals, their stakes — with your offer scoped to what was discussed and your real pricing, stated flat. No invented numbers, no unprompted discounts.
5. **Anytime: "who's waiting on what?"** The living pipeline — every open deal, whose move it is, what was promised and when. Your own overdue promises get flagged as loudly as their silence, and quiet deals get a suggested nudge.
6. **As calls accumulate, the coaching compounds.** Scorecards stack into trend coaching ("your win rate doubles when you set the next step before hanging up"), every objection you've ever heard builds into your own playbook with the answers that worked, and your prospects' verbatim words pile up as raw material for your marketing copy. It can even rehearse you — role-playing a real prospect from their own recorded words before a big call.

## What you end up with

A deal file on every prospect with their own words in it, a pipeline where nothing waits invisibly, follow-ups that go out the same hour instead of the same week, a growing objection playbook written by your real calls — and a sales coach with perfect memory telling you the one thing to fix next.

## What you need

- **Call transcripts** — from any recorder (Zoom, Fathom, Meet, Otter…), exported or pasted. Typed notes work too. No connector required.
- **Optional — a brand-voice skill** so drafts sound like you (works without one, using writing samples you point it at).
- **Optional — a profile** for your offers, pricing, and rhythm. It builds this with you on first run.
- **Works in both Cowork and Claude Code** — deal memory is plain files.

## Honest limits — and these matter

It works **after the call, not during it** — it never joins, records, or whispers in a live call (record calls in line with consent norms where you operate). It **drafts; it never sends** — every follow-up and proposal waits for you. It **never invents**: every quote traces to a transcript, every price traces to your profile, and if it doesn't know, it says so. And its coaching is honest — scores are earned, not inflated — because a coach that flatters you is just an expensive mirror.
