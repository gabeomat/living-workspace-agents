# Chart of Accounts Templates

Structural starting points for clients who don't have a Chart of Accounts (intake State B/C, "pick a template").
These are NOT a client's real books — they're scaffolds to adapt to the client's actual vendors and revenue.
If the client has an accountant-provided CoA, import that instead and don't override it.

Adapt any template to the client's real business: rename categories to match how they think, add categories for
their actual recurring vendors, remove ones they'll never use. The goal is a CoA they understand and will keep.

---

## Template 1 — Solo / Service Business (default for most cohort members)

Fits coaches, consultants, creators, freelancers, agencies of one.

**Income**
- Product/Program Revenue (courses, memberships, offers)
- Service Revenue (1:1, done-for-you, consulting)
- Affiliate / Partner Income
- Other Income
- (contra) Refunds & Chargebacks

**Expenses**
- Advertising & Marketing (ad spend, promo)
- Software & Subscriptions (tools, SaaS)
- Contractors & Freelancers
- Payment Processing Fees (Stripe, PayPal, etc.)
- Education & Coaching (courses, masterminds you're in)
- Office & Supplies
- Travel & Meals (business)
- Professional Services (legal, accounting)
- Bank & Finance Charges
- Other Business Expense

## Template 2 — Product / E-commerce Business

Adds cost-of-goods tracking.

**Income:** Product Sales · Wholesale · Shipping Collected · Other · (contra) Refunds
**Cost of Goods Sold:** Inventory/Product Cost · Shipping & Fulfillment · Packaging · Merchant Fees
**Expenses:** Advertising · Software & Subscriptions · Contractors · Professional Services · Office · Bank Charges · Other

---

## Template 3 — Local / Appointment-Based Business

Fits salons, studios, practitioners, local services.

**Income:** Service Revenue · Product/Retail Sales · Packages/Memberships · Other · (contra) Refunds
**Expenses:** Rent & Utilities · Supplies · Staff/Contractors · Advertising · Software & Booking Tools · Payment Fees · Insurance · Professional Services · Bank Charges · Other

---

## How to use
1. Offer the closest-fit template based on what the intake revealed about their business.
2. Adapt it live: pull their top vendors from the connected data and make sure each has a home category.
3. Save the adapted CoA into their bookkeeping spreadsheet + profile.
4. Reuse it every run — consistency is what makes month-over-month comparisons meaningful.
