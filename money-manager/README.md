# Money Manager

**Your AI bookkeeper and financial advisor in one. It keeps your books, shows you a branded profit-and-loss dashboard, and tells you where your money is going — without ever touching it.**

Money Manager connects to your financial data, keeps a clean set of books, and turns them into a clear picture of what you're actually making and where it's going. Then it does the part most bookkeepers don't: it advises. It flags unusual spend, warns when margins narrow, and reviews your recurring subscriptions for things worth cutting.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it meets you where you are.** A quick intake finds your starting point: you already have a bookkeeper and clean books (it **reconciles** and continues your structure), you have messy data (it **organizes** it), or you're starting from scratch (it **builds** your books). It settles your Chart of Accounts — imported from your accountant, or picked from a template and adapted to your business.
2. **It connects and cleans your books.** Pulls your transactions from your connected source and categorizes them by vendor into a clean bookkeeping spreadsheet — carefully, because it's built to catch the ways books quietly go wrong (miscategorized charges, hidden sub-accounts, double-counted transfers).
3. **It shows you where you stand.** A branded profit-and-loss dashboard — revenue, expenses, profit margin, month over month — in your brand colors. Open it any time to see your numbers at a glance.
4. **It advises.** On request (or when something jumps): flags unusual spend vs. last month, warns on narrowing margins, and gives you a subscription review — what you pay for, what's redundant, what's worth cutting — plus honest guidance on where your money should go.
5. **It keeps it current.** A monthly refresh pulls the latest, updates the books and dashboard, and tells you what changed.

## What you end up with
A clean, current set of books, a branded P&L dashboard you can pull up any time, and a straight-talking advisor that helps you spend smarter and keep more — so you always know your real profit margin and where the money's going.

## What you need
- **A connected financial source** — **Era** (recommended), **QuickBooks**, or any trusted financial MCP you connect to Claude. This is the one real requirement for automatic books. Without it, it works from a CSV export or figures you provide.
- **Optional — a profile** for your connector, Chart of Accounts, and brand colors. It builds this with you on first run.
- **Works in both Cowork and Claude Code** — the dashboard lives in Cowork as a view you keep; the heavier data work prefers Claude Code.

## Honest limits — and this one matters
Money Manager **analyzes and recommends. It never moves money, cancels a subscription, or files your taxes.** It tells you what it sees and what it would do; *you* make every financial move. It needs a connected data source (or your data) to work — it can't invent your finances. And it's a smart bookkeeping-and-advisory tool, not a licensed accountant or CPA.
