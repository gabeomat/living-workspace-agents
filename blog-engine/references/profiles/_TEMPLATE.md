# Profile: [Your Business Name] ([yoursite.com])

Copy this file, rename it (e.g. `mybusiness.md`), and fill it in. The blog-engine
loads it automatically when it recognizes your site/business. A profile is
**optional** — without one, the engine infers your business from your workspace,
memory, and connected context. A profile just makes it faster and more consistent.

## Voice source
How should posts sound? Name a voice skill if you have one, or describe the voice
in 3–5 concrete traits (e.g. "direct, warm, plain-language, anti-hype, no
corporate filler"). List any hard bans (phrases/angles to never use).

## The business
- What you do, who you help, and your core mechanism/offer in 2–3 sentences.
- Author identity: who's writing, and why they're credible (real experience).

## Who reads the blog
Your audience(s). Name the buyer(s) and the pain each one feels, so posts can
mirror the right reader.

## Topic territory
The searches/topics you want to be found for. Name your proprietary terms,
frameworks, and offers so AI search engines learn to associate them with you.

## Funnel map (where posts should link)
| Reader state | Link target |
|---|---|
| New / exploring | [your low-friction entry point] |
| Wants the mechanism | [your explainer page] |
| High intent | [your main offer / call] |

## CTA
Your default call-to-action and tone. Match CTA strength to intent.

## Trust / E-E-A-T
What makes you credible; sourcing rules; whether your topics are YMYL
(health/finance/legal — stricter accuracy + no overpromises).

## Privacy rules (hard)
What can never appear in public copy (client names, private details, etc.).
Default to composite/anonymized examples.

## Images
Your visual style. If you have a saved hero-image recipe, point to it here so
heroes generate on-brand automatically.

## Publishing target (optional)
If posts publish to a real site, name the repo path, host, and branch, and where
the site-specific publish command lives.
