# Blog Engine

**Give it a topic. Get a search-optimized, publish-ready blog post written in your voice — with a hero image.**

The Blog Engine is a full writing team in one agent. It doesn't just "write a blog post" — it researches what people are actually searching for, structures the post to rank in both Google and AI search, writes it in your voice, strips the tells of AI writing, and generates a matching hero image.

## What happens when you run it

1. **It learns your business.** Reads your workspace (and your profile, if you made one) so the post sounds like *you* and speaks to *your* audience — not a generic template.
2. **Keyword research.** Finds what your audience is really searching for and picks the angle with the best shot at being found.
3. **Structures for search + AI.** Builds the post for SEO *and* AEO/GEO — the way content gets surfaced in AI answers (Google, ChatGPT, Perplexity), including an FAQ section with proper schema.
4. **Writes the draft in your voice.** A complete, structured post — not an outline.
5. **Humanizes it.** Runs a pass to strip the classic AI-writing tells so it reads like a person wrote it.
6. **Adds a hero image.** Generates an on-brand featured image (or hands you the image prompt if no image tool is connected).

## What you end up with
A finished, publish-ready blog post in your voice, structured to be found in search and AI, with verified non-competing source links and a hero image. Paste it into your site and go.

## What you need
- **Claude** with this skill installed. Everything core (research, writing, link-checking) runs with no extra accounts.
- **Optional — a profile** (`references/profiles/_TEMPLATE.md`) to sharpen business/audience/voice. It also infers this from your workspace if you skip it.
- **Optional — an image tool** (`gpt-image-2` or `nano-banana`) for the automatic hero image. Without one, it hands you the image prompt to run yourself. Image generation never blocks the writing.

## Honest limits
It writes and optimizes — it doesn't publish to your site for you (you paste the finished post). Image generation depends on having an image tool connected; otherwise you get a ready-to-run prompt.
