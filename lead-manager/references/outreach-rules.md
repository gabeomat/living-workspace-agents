# Outreach rules — the non-negotiables

Read this before every drafting run. These rules exist because this agent messages real people on behalf of a real business owner, and one creepy, spammy, or fabricated message costs more trust than fifty good ones earn.

## 1. The agent never sends. Ever.

Every message is a draft in an approval batch. The human reads, edits if they want, and sends. The agent updates the ledger only after the user confirms what actually went out. No exceptions, no "it was just a thank-you note."

## 2. Reference what they SAID, never what you TRACKED

- ✅ Allowed: things the person chose to express in a context where being heard was the point — a question in an event chat, a community post, a reply, a survey answer, something they told the owner directly.
- ❌ Banned: inferred or tracked behavior. Never "I noticed you've been opening my emails," "I saw you clicked," "you've been quiet lately," or anything that reveals monitoring. Attentive, not surveilled — that's the line.

## 3. The recency cap

Quote or cite a signal only if it's within **60 days**. Older signals still inform scoring and the choice to reach out, but the message itself can't lean on them ("you asked about X in January…" lands as *we've been keeping a file on you*). If the best signal is old, the draft opens fresh instead.

## 4. Lead with usefulness

Every draft gives before it asks: answer the question they raised, share the resource that fits what they said, close the loop on the thing they mentioned. The ask comes after the give, and it's one ask — the routed ask-path for their segment — stated plainly. Never apologize for the offer; never bury it either.

## 5. Nudge limits

One follow-up if no response. After that, they rest until they act again (a new signal resets the clock). Anyone marked `not_interested` or `unsubscribed` is permanently untouchable unless the user personally reverses it.

## 6. Channel = where the relationship lives

Email someone who's on the email list. DM a community member inside the community. Message a social follower where they followed. Don't cold-jump channels ("found your personal cell") — that's the surveillance line again, from a different angle.

## 7. Consent and platform reality

- Email only people who opted in. This agent works from the user's own lists — it does not scrape, buy, or "enrich" contacts.
- Community/social DMs go only to people with an existing connection (member, follower, prior conversation), sent by the human. No automated sending, anywhere — most platforms ban it and it reads like it.

## 8. Match the segment to the ask

Cold → Entry offer (the low-commitment front door). Engaged → Core offer. Warm → Premium (or Core, per profile). **Never propose the high-ticket thing to someone with no engagement history** — it's both bad manners and bad conversion.

## 9. Voice: the owner on a good day

Drafts run through the user's brand-voice skill if installed, or their profile's voice notes. Short, specific, one person to one person. No broadcast-speak ("Hey there!"), no bot cadence, no template smell. If a draft could be pasted to anyone, it isn't personal — cut it or make it specific.

## 10. The 15-minute contract

The whole batch — default 10 people — must be approvable in one short sitting. If drafts routinely need heavy rewriting, that's a voice-tuning problem to fix (note what the user changes), not a reason to make the batch bigger or the messages longer. A system the owner can't clear in 15 minutes is a system that stops running.
