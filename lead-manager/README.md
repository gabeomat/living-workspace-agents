# Lead Manager

**The follow-up team you never had. It finds the warm people already in your world — your list, your community, your event attendees — remembers what they said, ranks who's warmest, and hands you a small weekly stack of personal messages ready to approve and send.**

Most businesses don't have a lead problem — they have an attention problem. You pay (in ad spend or in hours) to attract people, and then they sit: on the email list, in the community, in last month's workshop chat, saying things like *"how does pricing work?"* to no one. Lead Manager is the teammate whose whole job is making sure those people finally get a personal touch.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it maps who's already in your world.** A quick interview finds your pools (email list, community, events, past clients — one is enough to start), your offers (an entry offer for cold people, your core offer, your premium offer for warm people only), and your rhythm. Then it sets up your **Lead Ledger** — one file every source feeds into.
2. **You feed it exports; it builds the ledger.** Drop any export into the inbox folder — a subscriber CSV, a community member list, a webinar chat log — and it extracts every person into the ledger *once*, with their real words attached, dated. Raw files are never read twice, which is what keeps this fast and cheap. (Extraction runs on a lightweight model; the heavy thinking is saved for where it matters.)
3. **It scores who's actually warm — with rules, not vibes.** A deterministic script (no AI guessing) ranks everyone by what they've done: attended, asked, replied, joined, bought. Multi-pool people rank higher; warmth decays with time. Anyone it knows nothing about stays honestly cold — it never fakes familiarity.
4. **Every week: a small batch, ready to send.** The top ~10 eligible people, each with a drafted personal message in *your* voice that references what they actually said, routed to the right offer for how warm they are — cold people get the front-door invite, warm people get the premium conversation. You read, tweak, send. Fifteen minutes.
5. **It keeps the pipeline honest.** It tracks who replied, who's going cold, and what each batch produced — and anyone can be added by hand anytime ("add this person: …"), so side workflows and chance encounters feed the same ledger.

## What you end up with

One living Lead Ledger of everyone in your audience worth knowing, dossiers on your warmest prospects with their own words in them, and a weekly 15-minute ritual that turns people you already paid to attract into conversations — instead of letting them quietly expire.

## What you need

- **Your existing audience data** — any export you can download or paste: email list CSV, community member list, event attendee list, webinar chat log, past-client list. No connector required; one pool is enough to start.
- **Optional — a brand-voice skill** so drafts sound like you (it works without one, using writing samples you point it at).
- **Optional — a profile** for your pools, offers, and rhythm. It builds this with you on first run.
- **Works in both Cowork and Claude Code** — the ledger is plain files; scoring runs as a small local script.

## Honest limits — and this one matters

Lead Manager **drafts; it never sends.** Every message waits for you — that's a feature, because a personal touch sent by software isn't personal. It only references things people actually said in places they expected to be heard (never "I saw you opened my email" — it has rules about that). It works from *your* data — it doesn't scrape, buy, or "enrich" contacts. And it's honest about sparse data: people who've never engaged stay in your regular nurture instead of getting fake-personal messages.
