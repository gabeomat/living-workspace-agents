# Business Strategist

**The advisor who already read everything. Ask it anything strategic — or run its full review — and it sends fast, inexpensive analyst agents through your whole workspace (numbers, leads, pipeline, clients, goals), then turns their evidence into straight, push-back advice that cites the file every claim came from.**

Most strategy advice comes from people who haven't read anything — a coach who knows the story you told them, a friend who knows the vibe, an AI that knows nothing about your business. This advisor does the reading *first*. Then it gives it to you straight, including the parts you'd rather not hear.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it maps your business.** It scans your workspace and auto-detects what's already there — the pulse log, the lead ledger, the sales pipeline, the books, any tracker or spreadsheet you keep — then asks you to confirm where everything lives and tests each source with one real read. It captures your goals and your review cadence (quarterly by default), saves your source map, and immediately answers whatever brought you here.
2. **Anytime: ask it anything strategic.** *"Should I raise my price?" "Why is revenue flat?" "Is this new idea a distraction?"* It scopes the question to the domains it touches, dispatches a cheap analyst agent through each one — every analyst reading only its mapped sources — and answers from your real data. You get a position, the reasoning, the strongest argument against it, and a citation for every number.
3. **On a cadence: the full review.** All domains fan out at once, each analyst comparing against the last review's baseline. You get six sections: **what changed since last time → where the momentum is → where the leak is → last period's goals scored honestly → next period's plan capped at 3 priorities → the hard questions to sit with.** No grade inflation — "launched but zero sales" is not a hit.
4. **Every review saves a scorecard.** The next review reads it, so the advisor measures you against what you actually said last quarter — and gets smarter every time you run it.
5. **It pushes back.** If your new idea arrives right when the current plan got hard, it asks the one question — *is this moving your current goal forward, or is it next quarter's idea?* — names the tension once, then answers anyway. The decision stays yours.

## What you end up with

A strategic advisor who pushes back instead of cheerleading, never invents a number, and builds a quarter-over-quarter record of what you said you'd do versus what happened — plus everyday answers to hard questions that are grounded in your files, not in vibes.

## What you need

- **Anything you already track.** The more Library agents you run (daily-business-pulse, lead-manager, sales-call-copilot, money-manager), the more it has to read — but any spreadsheet, tracker, or notes file works, and no other agent is required.
- **Optional — a goals file** so reviews score progress against something concrete; it captures your goals in conversation if you have nothing.
- **Optional — a Business Brain / `CLAUDE.md`** so the advice understands your projects and history, not just your numbers.
- **Works in both Cowork and Claude Code** — parallel analyst agents where available, sequential reads everywhere else.

## Honest limits — and this one matters

The Strategist is **read-only**: it reads your files and your other agents' outputs; it writes only its own reviews and scorecards. It changes nothing, moves nothing, and sends nothing. It also never fakes certainty: a domain you don't track yet is reported as untracked — it becomes a "start tracking this" recommendation, never a guess and never a wall. Advice is only as good as the data you keep, and it will tell you when that data is thin. And it's an advisor, not a decision-maker: it takes a position and shows its evidence; you make the call.
