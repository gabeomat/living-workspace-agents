# Landing Page Builder

**Describe the page you need. Get a single, production-grade landing page in your brand and voice — one the agent renders in a real browser, critiques like a hostile design director, and rebuilds until the pixels are right.**

Tell it what page you want — a sales page, an opt-in, a workshop registration, a waitlist — and this agent art-directs it, writes conversion-grade copy in your voice, builds one self-contained page against a build standard it can't cut corners on, then does the thing most tools skip: it **renders the page, looks at its own screenshots, and fixes what it sees** — three passes — before handing you a live preview.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it learns your business.** A quick setup pulls your brand, voice, audience, and offer from your workspace (and asks only for what it can't find), then saves your profile. After that, every page just reads it.
2. **It locks the goal.** One page, one conversion action, and the *one thing this page must prove.* No fuzzy pages.
3. **It art-directs a real brief.** Concept, palette, typefaces, and one signature technique that makes the page memorable instead of generic — all drawn from your brand.
4. **It writes copy that converts, in your voice.** Real, final copy built on direct-response structure and buyer psychology — persuasive through clarity and belief, never hard-sell, never apologetic. If you have a brand-voice skill, it routes the copy through it so it sounds like you.
5. **It builds one self-contained page** to a non-negotiable standard: real copy, distinctive type, custom motion, responsive, accessible, zero console errors.
6. **It critiques its own pixels — three passes.** It renders the page (desktop + mobile, top to bottom), reads the screenshots like a mean art director — widows, weak contrast, dead zones, anything that smells like an AI default — fixes everything, and adds one deliberate craft upgrade each pass.
7. **It hands you a live preview.** You see it working in a browser, tweak anything, and it ships static to any host — on your say-so.

## What you end up with
One finished, self-contained landing page — in your brand, in your voice, written to convert — that has actually been rendered and design-reviewed against its own screenshots, ready to preview and deploy.

## What you need
- **Claude** with this skill installed. It pulls your brand, voice, audience, and offer from the workspace it runs in.
- **Recommended — a brand-voice skill** so the copy sounds like you (great copywriting structure either way; your voice on top when it's there).
- **Recommended — your brand colors, fonts, and any real media** (logo, headshots, product photos) available in the workspace. It never invents a face or a fake logo.
- **To run the screenshot-critique loop:** Playwright (headless Chrome). In Claude Code / the desktop app it installs automatically the first time (you just approve). Only a locked-down, no-code environment can't render — then it builds carefully and hands off the HTML, and tells you the critique passes couldn't run there.

## Honest limits
It builds **one great page at a time**, not a 25-site portfolio and not a full multi-page website (single pages are the point). It hands you a **live preview to ship**, not an auto-published page — the deploy decision stays yours. The self-critique loop needs a browser to render; without one you still get a carefully built page, just without the pixel-level review. It uses **real media only** — if a page needs imagery you don't have and no image tool is connected, it leans on procedural/CSS craft rather than inventing assets.
