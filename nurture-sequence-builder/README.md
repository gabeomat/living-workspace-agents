# Nurture Sequence Builder

**Your AI email funnel team. Tell it what funnel you're building — it designs the belief journey first, then writes every email to walk it, in your voice, powered by your real stories. Loaded into your email platform as drafts, or handed to you paste-ready.**

Funnels die in the follow-up. The opt-in page gets built, the event gets delivered — and the emails that were supposed to do the converting get written at 11pm the night before, or never. And when they do get written, they're usually the same emails for everyone, whether the reader just discovered they have a problem or has been circling the buy button for a month. This agent fixes both: the weeks of funnel-writing time, and the one-size-fits-nobody sequence.

## What happens when you run it

1. **First run only — it learns your business and banks your stories.** A quick interview captures your offers and real prices (sequences only ever sell these), your platform, your voice — and then the good part: it interviews you for your **real stories** (client wins, your origin, lessons learned), tags each by the belief it shifts, and files them in a story bank you'll grow forever. Most owners have never seen their own proof organized as an arsenal.
2. **Then, any time: "what kind of funnel are you building?"** Lead-magnet welcome, webinar/workshop follow-up (attendee, non-buyer, and no-show branches), launch, application funnel, re-engagement, post-purchase — or something custom to your business model. The funnel type sets the default architecture.
3. **It locates your reader before writing a word.** Where are they on the awareness ladder (problem-aware? solution-aware? ready?) and how warm are they? That position — not a template — decides how many emails, what each must accomplish, and how soon the ask comes. You approve the architecture *before* anything gets drafted.
4. **It asks for fresh stories, then writes the sequence.** Every email has one job and one CTA, written in your voice, using the stories whose belief-shift matches that email's checkpoint. New story or new offer next month? Same machine, fresh sequence — nothing is baked in.
5. **Delivery, your way.** If your email platform is connected to Claude, it creates the segment and loads every email as a **draft** — you flip the switch. No connection? You get a paste-ready package: every email, three subject-line variants each, and a plain-language timing plan you can build in any platform in one sitting.

## What you end up with

A finished, conversion-designed email sequence in hours instead of weeks; a strategy brief that makes every future edit cheap ("rewrite email 3" never re-does the thinking); and a story bank that compounds — every sequence you build makes the next one better armed.

## What you need

- **Your offers and real prices** — it sells what you actually sell.
- **3–5 real stories** — it pulls them out of you on first run; you add more anytime ("add a story").
- **Optional — a brand-voice skill** so emails sound like you (works without one, from writing samples).
- **Optional — your email platform connected** (via a Claude connector or MCP) if you want segments and drafts loaded for you. Whether a connection exists for your platform is yours to check — without one you still get everything, paste-ready.
- **Works in both Cowork and Claude Code** — the story bank and sequences are plain files.

## Honest limits — and these matter

It **never sends or activates anything** — drafts and segments only; your hand flips the switch. It **never invents**: every story, result, and number comes from what you actually told it, and deadlines only appear when they're real — no fake countdowns, no manufactured scarcity, because a list that catches you lying once never believes a deadline again. It writes for people who **opted in** — it won't write for purchased or scraped lists. And it will tell you when the sequence you asked for isn't the one you need (a list that's been cold for six months gets a re-engagement recommendation before a pitch sequence, every time).
